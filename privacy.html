Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 15, 2026

Short version: GitAfterIt is a client for your own GitHub account. Your credentials and content stay on your device. We don't run analytics servers, sell data, or track you.

What GitAfterIt stores

  • Authentication. When you sign in, GitAfterIt uses GitHub's official OAuth device flow (or a personal access token you provide). Tokens are stored in the iOS Keychain on your device and are never sent anywhere except GitHub.
  • Your GitHub content. Repositories, files, pull requests, and other data are fetched directly from GitHub's API to your device and cached locally to make the app fast and available offline. This cache lives on your device.
  • App settings. Preferences such as pinned repositories, hosts, and layout are stored locally on your device (and in your private iCloud/app-group container where you enable it).

Who your data is shared with

  • GitHub. The app talks directly to GitHub's REST and GraphQL APIs using your credentials, subject to GitHub's Privacy Statement.
  • Servers you connect to. If you open an SSH terminal or connect to your own hosts, the app communicates with those machines directly, as you direct it to.
  • No one else. GitAfterIt includes no third-party advertising or analytics SDKs. We do not operate a backend that collects your data.

Coding agents

When you launch or monitor Copilot coding agents, GitAfterIt reads agent session data from the machines you connect to (locally or over SSH you control) and, where you use it, from GitHub Copilot. Agent activity is governed by GitHub Copilot's terms and privacy.

Data you can remove

Signing out clears your tokens from the Keychain. Deleting the app removes all locally cached content and settings from your device.

Children

GitAfterIt is a developer tool and is not directed to children under 13.

Changes

If this policy changes, we'll update the date above and post the revised policy on this page.

Questions about this policy? Email privacy@gitafterit.app.
